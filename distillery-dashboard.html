Process Improvement Insights

Key Observations

  • Fermentations with initial gravity below 1.050 show more consistent attenuation curves
  • Second nutrient addition at 48 hours correlates with improved fermentation completion rates
  • Most fermentations reach terminal gravity (1.015 or below) within 96 hours
  • Temperature control is a critical factor in fermentation consistency
  • Batches with higher initial gravity (>1.065) take approximately 20% longer to complete

Recommendations

  • Maintain initial gravity between 1.055-1.060 for optimal balance between yield and fermentation time
  • Continue split nutrient additions with 1.0g/gal initially and 0.3g/gal at 48 hours
  • Implement cooling system for summer fermentations to maintain consistent temperature profile
  • Standardize initial pitch rate at 2.0g/gal for all fermentations
  • Schedule stripping runs for 120 hours after yeast pitch for most consistent results

Production Insights

Key Production Insights

  • Queen's Share runs (QS-1, QS-2) yield approximately 50% more hearts volume than standard runs
  • Queen's Share runs maintain higher ABV throughout the run (80-83% vs 70-76%)
  • Standard runs typically yield 31-35 gallons of hearts with 2.5-3.0 gallons of heads collection
  • Production improved from April through December 2024, with runs #25-33 showing better efficiency
  • ABV profiles show consistent degradation patterns across all runs, suggesting stable distillation techniques
  • Summer runs (#15-20) show slightly higher initial ABV values, likely due to ambient temperature differences
  • Recent runs (#30-33) show optimized cuts with better hearts yields compared to early runs (#9-14)
