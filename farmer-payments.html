Analysis of payments to local sugarcane farmers

Farmer Payment Analysis

This visualization tracks payments made to our network of local sugarcane farmers throughout Dominica. Our data shows the distribution across different villages and individual farmers, highlighting our commitment to fair compensation and supporting local agriculture.

Village Distribution

The data reveals that La Plaine is the dominant growing region, accounting for approximately 33% of all payments. The top three villages (La Plaine, Belles, and Wesley) represent about 60% of our total payments to farmers, demonstrating our focus on regions with ideal growing conditions for high-quality sugarcane.

Farmer Insights

When examining individual farmer data, we see that the top three farmers (all from La Plaine) account for approximately 35% of all payments. This concentration reflects both the larger farm sizes in La Plaine and the exceptional quality of cane produced by these experienced growers.

Key Takeaways:

  • La Plaine is the dominant village with approximately $76,000 in payments
  • The top 3 villages account for about 60% of all payments
  • Within villages, there's typically one lead farmer who receives significantly more than others
  • La Plaine has both the highest total payments and the highest individual farmer payments
  • Smaller villages like Petite Soufriere have fewer farmers but relatively high per-farmer averages
